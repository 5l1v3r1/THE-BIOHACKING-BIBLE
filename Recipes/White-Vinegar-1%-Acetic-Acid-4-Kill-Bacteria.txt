Many of the white vinegars on the market are produced by mixing acetic acid with water.

After having analyzed it it was discovered that it has a concentration of 5.81%.

https://www.chimica-online.it/test/esercizio-equivalenti-11.htm

Many bacteria, such as Pseudomonas aeruginosa, are sensitive to an acetic acid solution, in a concentration 
not higher than 1% it is possible to use it directly on the skin.
For direct experiments on bacteria it is obviously possible to use higher concentrations ....


Recipe for 100 ml of 1% Acetic Acid (about)

94,2 ml Distilled Water
